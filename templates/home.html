Understanding the SWAYAM Platform

SWAYAM (Study Webs of Active-Learning for Young Aspiring Minds) is the national online learning platform of the Government of India, offering credit-bearing courses aligned with NEP 2020.

Eligibility

  • All UG and PG students eligible
  • Use official university email ID
  • Local Chapter ID: 7730

Registration

  • Visit www.swayam.gov.in
  • Sign in with university email
  • Select JAIN – Chapter 7730

Evaluation

  • 70% End-term examination
  • 30% Internal assessment
  • Min 40% in each for credits

Credit Transfer

  • Recorded in Academic Bank of Credits
  • Reflected in mark sheet
  • NEP 2020 aligned

Course Identification & Approval Process

1
Departments identify relevant credit-bearing SWAYAM courses aligned with SAMUDI categories
2
Proposed courses scrutinized and recommended by Department Board of Studies (BoS)
3
Forwarded to Office of Academics for compliance review and Academic Council approval

Roles & Responsibilities

SWAYAM Coordinator

  • Coordinate student registration
  • Monitor student participation
  • Track progress reports

Office of Academics

  • Policy formulation
  • Academic Council coordination
  • Quality assurance

NEP Saarthi – Student Leaders Driving Academic Transformation

NEP SAARTHIS are student volunteers who assist in implementing NEP 2020 across the university by spreading awareness and supporting academic transformation initiatives. They serve as the bridge between the Office of Academics and the student community.

Roles and Responsibilities

  • Conduct orientation sessions on NEP 2020 framework for incoming students
  • Promote multidisciplinary electives and open courses across departments
  • Organise Indian Knowledge System workshops and cultural events
  • Serve as liaison between students and Office of Academics
  • Mentor junior students in course selection and credit planning

Selection Criteria

  • Second year and above students with CGPA ≥ 7.5
  • Strong communication and leadership skills demonstrated through prior activities
  • Commitment for one full academic year with regular participation
  Current Cohort: 45 NEP SAARTHIS active across 12 departments

  Apprenticeship Embedded Degree Programme (AEDP)

UGC Guidelines for Higher Educational Institutions – 2025. Bridging academic rigour with real-world industry practice.

NEP 2020Policy Alignment
10Credits / 3 Months
TripartiteHEI + Industry + Student
All UGProgrammes

Introduction & Policy Context

NEP 2020 emphasises experiential learning and integration of academic knowledge with vocational training. AEDP integrates structured apprenticeship training within undergraduate programmes to bridge classroom learning with industry requirements, enabling students to earn academic credits through real-world work experience.

AEDP Definition: A programme enabling a student to undertake apprenticeship training as part of the degree curriculum as per the Apprentices Act 1961 and Apprenticeship Rules 2019, with credits stored in the Academic Bank of Credits (ABC).

Duration & Credit Mechanism

Programme Type Minimum Duration Maximum Duration
UG – 3 Years 1 Semester 3 Semesters
UG – 4 Years 2 Semesters 4 Semesters

10 Credits

Per 3-month apprenticeship spell

NCrF Framework

Credits mapped to National Credit Recognition Framework

ABC Portal

All credits stored in Academic Bank of Credits

MEME

Supports Multiple Entry Multiple Exit pathways

Roles of Stakeholders

  BOAT / BOPT

Facilitate industry partnerships and ensure regulatory compliance

  Higher Educational Institution

Design curriculum, supervise, and upload credits to ABC

  Industry / Establishment

Provide structured workplace training and monthly stipend

  Student / Apprentice

Complete training, maintain logbook, and submit final reports

Assessment Methodology

Assessment follows a blended model ensuring both industry performance and academic rigour are captured comprehensively.

Industry Evaluation

30–40% — assessed by the workplace supervisor on task performance

Faculty Mentor

30–40% — evaluated via periodic site visits and progress reports

Seminar & Viva

20–40% — final presentation and oral examination at the institution

Consequences of Non-Compliance

HEIs failing to adhere to AEDP guidelines as prescribed by UGC may face the following punitive actions:

  • Debarred from UGC schemes and funding
  • Debarred from offering new degree programmes
  • Debarred from ODL and online programme offerings
  • Removed from the list of HEIs recognised under the UGC Act
